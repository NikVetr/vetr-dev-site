# Research memo: Stephen Montgomery Lab

_Last updated for this scaffold: 2026-04-02_

## Purpose

This memo summarizes public-facing facts and positioning inputs that should guide the design and content strategy for the new Montgomery Lab website.

## Core public positioning

The current Montgomery Lab public sites repeatedly frame the lab around gene regulation, gene expression, and the path from genes to cells to disease.

### Public positioning signals

- The Stanford Montgomery Lab homepage describes Stephen Montgomery as studying how and why genes turn on and off and vary from person to person.
- The current GitHub-hosted lab site uses the phrase **“From genes to cells to diseases.”**
- Stanford profile pages describe Montgomery as best known for mapping the effects of genetic variation to gene expression and for pioneering the use of molecular outliers to identify impactful rare variants.

## PI roles and affiliations visible publicly

Public Stanford pages identify Stephen B. Montgomery as:
- Endowed Professor of Pathology
- Professor of Genetics
- Professor of Biomedical Data Science
- by courtesy, Professor of Computer Science

Public Stanford profiles also list affiliations including:
- Bio-X
- Cardiovascular Institute
- Wu Tsai Human Performance Alliance
- Maternal & Child Health Research Institute

## Publicly visible research emphases

Across the Stanford profile and lab pages, the lab is publicly associated with:
- gene regulation and gene expression
- genetic effects on cellular phenotypes
- functional genomics
- rare and environment-responsive variation
- structural variation
- understudied RNAs
- rare disease / undiagnosed disease
- computational and statistical genomics
- exercise molecular biology / molecular effects of training

## Publicly visible milestone signals

Public Stanford profile text highlights:
- early work connecting whole genomes and transcriptomes within a human population
- pioneering molecular outlier approaches for impactful rare variants
- 2017 GTEx analyses of genetic effects on gene expression across tissues
- 2024 MoTrPAC analyses of molecular effects of exercise training across rat tissues

## Design implications

### What the current public presence already proves
- The science is strong.
- The consortium network is large.
- The PI profile is well established.
- There is enough substantive material to support a high-end custom site.

### What the public presence under-expresses
- lab scale and energy
- team culture and mentorship
- translational arc from variant to function to disease
- software/resource identity
- recruitment pathways
- visual distinction from standard institutional templates

## Tone recommendation

The new site should feel:
- modern
- scientific
- sharp
- current
- visually ambitious
- collaborative
- credible
- human

It should **not** feel:
- corporate biotech
- generic academic CMS
- solemn or ceremonial
- “old money institutional”
- dependent on Stanford template styling

## Practical recommendation

The site should lead with the lab’s scientific program and collective identity, with Stephen Montgomery as the anchor rather than the sole subject.
